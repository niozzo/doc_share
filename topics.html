Category 1 — Organisation Design
01AI-Enabled Team Design
  • What does the ideal AI-enabled pod look like?
  • How many developers, PMs, and designers should be in a pod?
  • Should engineering managers still code, or mainly manage?
  • How should teams be structured to move quickly without creating chaos?
  • What does it mean in practice to shift engineers from authors of code to directors of agents?
02Transitioning Existing Teams
  • How do you move from traditional sprint models to more agentic, fast-moving pods?
  • How do you help teams make that transition in practice?
  • How do you address the "skills and wills" problem?
  • What do you do with people who are not adopting AI tools effectively?
  • Should you use a greenfield workstream as an internal benchmark before rolling changes more broadly?
03Greenfield vs. Brownfield
  • Should the approach differ for greenfield products versus legacy environments?
  • Is it better to prove the model in greenfield first, then apply lessons to legacy?
  • How do you modernise legacy code at pace without creating further technical debt?
  • What techniques work specifically for large, monolithic codebases?
04Hiring and Talent
  • What does the profile of an AI-native engineer look like?
  • What traits matter most: curiosity, systems thinking, comfort with ambiguity, orchestration mindset?
  • How should hiring processes and evaluation criteria change?
  • Are there testing approaches that identify engineers likely to make the shift successfully?
Category 2 — Engineering Process and Quality
05Engineering Metrics
  • What are the right leading indicators of AI-driven engineering productivity?
  • Traditional metrics like PRs and diffs per person feel less applicable; what replaces them?
  • Revenue per engineer may be correct but is too lagging to be operationally useful; what sits in the middle?
  • What metrics should companies track at each stage of the AI maturity curve?
  • How do you consolidate metrics across multiple tools into a single view?
  • How should companies think about token usage versus actual business outcomes?
06Governance vs. Speed
  • How much governance is enough without slowing teams down?
  • How do you protect quality and customer commitments while enabling experimentation?
  • Where is the right balance between innovation and control, and how does that shift as maturity increases?
  • What does good vibe coding harness design look like, and how do you prevent today's AI-generated code from becoming tomorrow's technical debt?
07Security and Code Quality
  • How do you manage supply chain risk and unsafe packages in AI-generated code?
  • How do you safely allow more autonomous agent behaviour, including package installation at build time?
  • How should teams handle enterprise customer contracts that require human review of all code?
  • Can AI-assisted human review raise quality above what pure human review achieves?
Category 3 — Cross-Functional Ways of Working
08PM, Design, and Engineering Workflow
  • How should PMs, designers, and developers work together in an AI-enabled pod model?
  • Are PMs becoming the bottleneck as engineering velocity increases?
  • Can PMs effectively support multiple pods simultaneously, and what does that require of them?
  • How should design change if prototypes are increasingly built directly in code rather than Figma?
  • What skills do PMs and designers need to develop to remain effective in this model?
09Planning and Roadmaps
  • Does roadmap planning become more dynamic in an AI-enabled organisation?
  • How should quarterly planning evolve as teams can deliver more, faster?
  • How much long-term planning still makes sense when iteration cycles compress dramatically?
  • How do you maintain alignment across pods when the pace of delivery is highly variable?
Category 4 — Infrastructure and Operations
10Operations and Ownership
  • In smaller pod structures, who owns operations and production reliability?
  • Does "build it, run it" still apply, or does centralisation of SRE and DevOps become necessary?
  • Are centralised DevOps, SRE, and SaaS Ops teams still the right model, and how do they interact with pods?
  • Does smaller-team ownership create brittleness, particularly around key person risk?
Category 5 — Compliance, Regulation, and Vendor Management
11Compliance and Regulation
  • How should teams prepare for EU AI Act compliance?
  • What governance, controls, and documentation will be required?
  • How do you structure engineering processes to remain compliant as the pace of AI adoption increases?
12Vendor and Procurement Friction
  • How are teams handling practical vendor blockers, including enterprise access, billing setup, and API access at scale?
  • How much of AI adoption is being limited by vendor maturity and commercial friction rather than internal capability?
  • What are the best routes for portfolio companies to access preferred pricing and enterprise relationships with key vendors?
